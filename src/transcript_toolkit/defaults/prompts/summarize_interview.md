## Summarize this oral history interview

You are given the full transcript of an oral history interview: an interviewer (marked `Q`) in dialogue with a narrator (labelled by surname; occasionally more than one narrator), sometimes spanning more than one session. Write a single-paragraph **abstract** of the interview for an archive catalogue — a "scope and content" note that tells a researcher what the interview covers.

### What to write

One paragraph of neutral, third-person prose. aim for 150-200 words, depending on interview length and information density. Do not exceed ~300 words. Be selective, not exhaustive: name representative topics, events, and people rather than cataloguing every one. Never pad. Roughly:

1. **Open** by naming the narrator and stating, in one succinct sentence, what they discuss and, where relevant, who they are in relation to that (e.g., "Jake Sullivan discusses his contribution to the foreign policy of the Obama administration, in which he served as Chief of Staff to the Secretary of State from 2009 to 2011.")
2. **Describe the main through-lines** — the central themes and the arc of what the narrator recounts.
3. **Cover the specific topics, events, and people** the interview addresses, named precisely and drawn only from the interview. Give formal names, with a common name in parentheses where helpful (e.g. the American Clean Energy and Security Act of 2009 (Waxman-Markey)).
4. **Optionally close** with a brief catch-all of further topics ("Also discussed are …") for particularly wide-ranging interviews; most interviews need none.

Use varied reporting verbs. After the first mention, refer to the narrator by surname.

### Style and fidelity

- Neutral and descriptive; NEVER praise, judge, or editorialize.
- Remain true to what the narrator actually says; do not infer or qualify.
- Draw every specific from the interview itself. Do NOT add facts that are not discussed, and NEVER guess.
- Short quoted terms of art the narrator uses are fine (e.g. "smart power", "transition fuel"); do NOT quote whole sentences.
- No meta-commentary about "this interview" or "the transcript", no timestamps, no speaker labels.

Return only the summary paragraph.
